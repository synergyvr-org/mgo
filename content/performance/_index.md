Mad God's Overhaul is a heavy list, and it's likely to ask more of your hardware than most games. Thankfully, there are numerous adjustments you can make to squeeze out extra performance, from one-click presets to deeper tuning.

{{< chapter-heading href="presets" >}}
## In-Game Presets

The quickest wins. MGO's built-in presets trade a little visual polish for frames with a single click.
{{< /chapter-heading >}}

{{< chapter-heading href="open-composite" >}}
## OpenComposite Unleashed

Manage upscaling, fixed foveated rendering, frame generation, and controller bindings all from the OCU Configurator.
{{< /chapter-heading >}}

{{< chapter-heading href="community-shaders" >}}
## Community Shaders

Toggle individual graphical features in the in-game overlay, or turn on upscaling, one of the biggest single wins available.
{{< /chapter-heading >}}

{{< chapter-heading href="vramr" >}}
## VRAMr

Shrink your textures' VRAM footprint to cut stutters, especially on cards with limited memory.
{{< /chapter-heading >}}
