Skyrim VR shipped with a "Realistic Bow Aiming" option, and it was actually pretty good! Of course it still wasn't good enough for the sickos who make mods. MGO includes a few mods to make archery (and whatever you call "using a crossbow") even better.

## Archery

With _aiming_ being pretty good in vanilla, the community needed other things to obsess over. They found plenty.

In vanilla, an arrow remains in your dominant hand after each shot. You have to hold {{< control name="trigger" >}} as you nock each arrow, but you never have to grab a _new_ arrow. With {{< nexus 28524 >}}Simple Realistic Archery VR{{< /nexus >}}, you need to reach behind your shoulder and grab a new arrow with {{< control name="trigger" >}} (or another button that you configure in the MCM[^1]) after each shot. {{< nexus 117254 >}}Seamless Arrow Nocking VR{{< /nexus >}} allows you to keep {{< control name="trigger" >}} held down as you nock the new arrow, rather than needing to squeeze it a second time.

{{< nexus 20364 >}}Haptic Skyrim VR{{< /nexus >}} adds haptic feedback for drawing your bow, among other things.

{{< nexus 169833 >}}Pull Arrows VR - Immersive Extraction{{< /nexus >}} lets you recover your arrow by pulling them out of whatever (or whoever) they're stuck in. Grab a stuck arrow with your hand, and pull it free. Pull them from walls, trees, bandits, and friends! The mod also adds some material-appropriate sound effects. It even lets you stab people with arrows.

With {{< nexus 160603 >}}Ricochet - Arrow Physics Framework{{< /nexus >}}, arrows and bolts glance off of hard or angled surfaces and bounce around. You can tune its behavior in the included MCM.

{{< nexus 105622 >}}Scared of Shootings{{< /nexus >}} does exactly what its subtitle promises: NPCs react to having a bow aimed at them. Draw down on someone and they'll notice, and they won't be thrilled about it. Now you're a deadly force lurking in the treeline, not a weirdo pointing a longbow at a shopkeeper who couldn't care less.

## Crossbows

The "stealth archer" play style is a cliché at this point, but modders haven't forgotten the crossbow-wielding vampire slayers out there. In vanilla, a crossbow reloads itself: after each shot, a fresh bolt simply materializes in place and the cocking lever pulls itself back, whether you like it or not. It's convenient, but is convenience _immersive_? I didn't think so.

{{< nexus 139152 >}}Immersive Crossbow Reload VR{{< /nexus >}} makes you _earn_ your reloads. It has a pretty big (but good, if you ask me) impact on balance. After each shot, grab a bolt from your lower back with {{< control name="trigger" >}}, bring it to the crossbow to seat it, then grab the cocking mechanism[^2] and pull it back until the string locks. If the default buttons don't suit you, you can remap bolt-grabbing and lever-pulling separately in the mod's INI file.
