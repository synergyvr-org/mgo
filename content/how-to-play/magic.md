Vanilla magic was arguably not as disappointing as vanilla melee combat, but it left something to be desired. You point your hand, you hold a trigger, fire comes out. It works, but for a game that lets you _be a freaking wizard in VR_, it doesn't quite deliver on the fantasy. Naturally, modders couldn't leave it alone. Mad God's Overhaul leans on a pile of mods to make spellcasting feel more like... well, _magic_.

## Selecting spells

In vanilla, swapping what's equipped means diving into menus, which is a real momentum killer. {{< nexus 47630 >}}Spell Wheel VR{{< /nexus >}} fixes that with a radial menu you can open with a button combo, letting you swap spells (and shouts, and items) on the fly. For the full tour, see the [Spell Wheel VR](/mod-highlights/spell-wheel) page.

## Casting

Several mods work together to make the actual _casting_ feel better in VR.

{{< nexus 55751 >}}Magic Improvements for Skyrim VR{{< /nexus >}} makes casting look better in several ways, including better dual-casting, improved aiming, and particle effects that scale according to your remaining magicka. {{< nexus 92804 >}}Dual Casting Fix VR{{< /nexus >}} fixes a bug that made dual-casting behave like single-casting in VR.

{{< nexus 119689 >}}Spell Auto-Aim VR{{< /nexus >}} gives your projectiles a little help finding their mark. Its default settings are fairly subtle, and it's configurable with an [INI](/reference/editing-inis).

{{< nexus 164902 >}}SpellBender VR{{< /nexus >}}, another of Machina's many mods in the list, lets you steer magical projectiles. It's very cool, and it's configurable with an  [INI](/reference/editing-inis). Check out the {{< nexus 164902 >}}Nexus page{{< /nexus >}} for details.

## Weapon infusions

{{< nexus 58023 >}}Conduit - Short-term Weapon Infusions{{< /nexus >}} lets you temporarily infuse your weapon with the effect of a spell you have equipped in your other hand. It combines with existing enchantments, and it even works on bound weapons. Conducted effects work with [Weapon Throw VR](/mod-highlights/weapon-throw) too!

## Out with the old

Collect enough spellbooks and your spell list gets unwieldy fast. {{< nexus 51125 >}}Forget Spell{{< /nexus >}} and {{< nexus 15558 >}}Spell Organizer{{< /nexus >}} let you hide or remove spells you'll never cast again (or ever) so they stop cluttering up your menu.

## In with the new

{{< nexus 27839 >}}Mysticism - A Magic Overhaul{{< /nexus >}} rebalances Skyrim's magic system and adds new spells across every school (while bringing back some fan-favorite Elder Scrolls spells of yore). {{< nexus 95196 >}}Sorcerer{{< /nexus >}} does the same for staves and scrolls, and {{< nexus 91602 >}}Arcane{{< /nexus >}} adds even more spells (and one power) to help a mage build stays viable throughout your run.

{{< aside type="btw" title="Spellsiphon" >}}
For a more radical overhaul, {{< nexus 26627 >}}Spellsiphon - Immersive Combat{{< /nexus >}} mod (found in the **OPTIONAL Mods** section and disabled by default) turns magic into a new, fast, resource-juggling combat system. It's a big departure from vanilla, but people who love it _really_ love it.
{{< /aside >}}
