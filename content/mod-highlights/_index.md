MGO bundles many hundreds of mods, and most of them just do their thing and require no individual attention from the player (that's you). A few, however, require some explanation. You can't take full advantage of features if you don't know that they exist.

{{< chapter-heading href="vrik" >}}
## VRIK Player Avatar

A full, animated body in VR, plus body holsters, a gesture system, and selfie mode.
{{< /chapter-heading >}}

{{< chapter-heading href="spell-wheel" >}}
## Spell Wheel VR

A radial menu for wrangling spells, shouts, and items without ever pausing the game.
{{< /chapter-heading >}}

{{< chapter-heading href="weapon-throw" >}}
## Weapon Throw VR

Throw any equipped weapon with optional auto-return, like the cross sub-weapon in Castlevania!

<small>Yes, also like Thor—but that's the comparison everyone else uses, so it bores me.</small>
{{< /chapter-heading >}}

{{< chapter-heading href="dovavr" >}}
## DovaVR Locomotion (optional)

Walk, swim, and ride by swinging your arms instead of pushing a thumbstick.
{{< /chapter-heading >}}

{{< chapter-heading href="fake-edge" >}}
## Fake Edge VR (optional)

Weapon collisions! Amaze your friends! Terrify your foes! Bump your blades into walls, tables, shields, and each other.
{{< /chapter-heading >}}
